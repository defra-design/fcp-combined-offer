11:30am		 The land parcels chosen for action CAB12, which involve providing supplementary winter bird food, require amendments to meet the necessary criteria for approval by Natural England. This adjustment is essential for the application process. Firstly, the current state of the land parcels may not support the intended purpose of providing supplementary winter bird food. Soil tests should be conducted to evaluate nutrient levels and soil structure. If the soil lacks essential nutrients or has poor structure, it may not support the growth of plants that produce the necessary seeds and grains for winter bird food. Secondly, the existing vegetation on the land parcels may not be conducive to attracting and supporting winter bird populations. The types of plants currently present may not produce the seeds and grains needed for supplementary bird food. It is important to identify and introduce plant species that are known to provide high-quality food for birds during the winter months. Engaging with local agricultural advisers or consultants can provide valuable insights and recommendations for making the necessary amendments. Their expertise can help identify potential issues and suggest effective solutions to ensure compliance with Natural England’s standards. By addressing these specific requirements, conducting thorough assessments, and implementing sustainable management practices, the application can demonstrate a strong commitment to environmental stewardship and sustainable agriculture.
